Greek Cabonara Recipe

Description

One of the most famous Italian dishes (besides pizza) but with a twist!

The Greek style carbonara has some key differences from the original, as you can see below.

Ingredients

In order to prepare this dish, you are going to need the following:

  • 500 gr spaghetti
  • 150 gr. bacon, slices
  • 150 gr. champinion mushrooms
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 330 gr. light cream

    • To serve

  • 150 gr. parmesan cheese, grated
  • pepper, freshly ground
  • parsley, finely chopped

Steps

  1. Place a pot over high heat, fill it with water, and bring it to a boil.
  2. Add some salt and the pasta, and boil it according to the packet’s instructions.
  3. Stack the bacon slices together and cut them into thin strips.
  4. Place a frying pan over high heat and let it heat up well.
  5. Add the olive oil and the bacon to the pan. (If the bacon is quite fatty, you will not need the olive oil.)
  6. Add the mushrooms and sauté them for 4-5 minutes until nicely browned.
  7. Add the whole clove of garlic and the heavy cream, and mix well.
  8. Allow 1-2 minutes for the sauce to come to a boil. (You do not want the sauce to thicken a lot at this point).
  9. Remove the pot from the heat, drain the pasta, and reserve the water.
  10. Add the pasta to the pan and mix well. (If the sauce needs more moisture, add a little of the pasta water you reserved.)
  11. Take out the garlic and remove the pan from the heat.
  12. Divide the pasta among plates and serve with grated cheese, freshly ground pepper, and finely chopped parsley.

Bon Appetit!
